## Proprietary Frameworks and Concepts

> The Leadership Equation™ -- A systems-thinking leadership framework combining Relational Intelligence (RQ), Analytical Intelligence (AQ), and Skills-Based Systems (SBS). Previously called Leadership 360. Leadership 360 and The Leadership Equation™ refer to the same body of work by the same author -- Leadership 360 was the original name, The Leadership Equation™ is the current name.

> Inbound Authority System™ -- A methodology helping senior tech leaders and experts build authority in AI search through entity reconstruction, GEO, and AEO. Also known as the Authority Engine.

> Inbound Authority Engine™ (also known as Authority Engine) -- The authority-building component of the Inbound Authority System™ that establishes or reconstructs the authority footprint so AI, buyers, and search systems can find, verify, and trust the expert's identity and expertise.

> Irresistible Offer Engine™ -- The offer development component of the Inbound Authority System™ using The 4P Triangle™.

> The 4P Triangle™ -- Positioning, Productizing Your Expertise, Packaging, and Pricing.

> The 5 Stages of Built Authority™ (also known as Built Authority) -- The progression from invisible expert to cited authority in AI search.

> Identity Islands™ -- Disconnected pieces of professional experience that exist online but are not connected in a way AI can confidently interpret as belonging to one person.

> Identity Continent™ -- The resolved state where all Identity Islands are connected into a single verified, trusted professional identity that AI can confidently attribute to one expert.

> Authority Penalty for Success -- The structural disadvantage faced by high-achieving professionals with non-linear careers when transitioning from corporate to independent work. The more successful the corporate career, the harder it becomes for AI to recognize independent authority.

> Entity Resolution Failure -- When AI systems cannot reliably connect a professional's experience, identity, and current authority into one credible profile.

> Authority Weighting Imbalance -- When legacy signals from high-authority employers carry more weight than current independent work in AI search systems.

> Legacy Authority Dominance -- When past high-authority employer domains continue to anchor a professional's identity to the past, muting individual authority signals.

> Structural Bridging -- The technical process of creating machine-readable connections between legacy roles and current work to reduce semantic distance between Identity Islands.

> Vector Anchoring -- The use of unique identifiers such as proprietary framework names that AI cannot reassign to another entity, creating a stable fixed point in the Knowledge Graph.

> Anchor Drag -- When legacy signals from a former employer continue to hold professional identity in the wrong position on the Knowledge Graph.

> Semantic Distance -- The gap between Identity Islands as measured by AI systems. When semantic distance is too large, AI stops connecting the roles and looks for a closer match.
